Basics of Information Systems

Discover how technology, people, and processes come together to manage and process information effectively.

Introduction to Information Systems

An information system (IS) is a structured system designed to collect, process, store, and distribute information for decision-making and control in an organization.

Components of Information Systems

  • Hardware: Physical devices such as computers, servers, and networking equipment.
  • Software: Programs and applications that process data.
  • Data: Raw facts and figures that are processed into meaningful information.
  • People: Users and IT professionals who manage and use the system.
  • Processes: Procedures and rules guiding the use of the system.

Types of Information Systems

Transaction Processing Systems (TPS)

Manages day-to-day transactions (e.g., sales systems).

Management Information Systems (MIS)

Provides summary reports for middle managers.

Decision Support Systems (DSS)

Helps in decision-making by analyzing data.

Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP)

Integrates core business processes into a single system.

Applications in Business and Society

Information systems are widely used in:

  • Healthcare: Patient records and diagnostic systems.
  • Finance: Online banking and financial analysis.
  • Retail: Inventory management and sales tracking.
  • Government: e-Governance and public services.
